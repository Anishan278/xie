Pros and Cons of owning a Cat

Pros

Does not take up lots of space for a house pet

Cats are very independent animals and small animals, so most
of the time they roam around and a lot of the time they might
not even be in the house! This makes it so that even if you have
to get them food or make sure they have a proper sleeping area it
wouldn’t take as much space as a dog or maybe even a bird.





Are very soft and cuddly

Cats can offer much support by just being around. If you ever need
to hug something or you’re feeling lonely you can just cuddle with
a cat and most of them wouldn’t mind!











Can be a comfort animal and support you emotionally

Cats can often be used as support emotionally. (More on that in another page.)












They are entertaining

Cats can often be used as support emotionally. (More on that in another page.)
Cats are entertaining because they love to play and they sometimes just do their
own thing. When you shine a lazer many of them try to chase after it, also they can
jump very high. Many times people buy kitty toys so that they can mess around with the
cat, however this is not needed. But sometimes just witnessing the bizarre things they
pull of is very entertaining to make your day!











They are independant

Cats don’t really depend on their owner a lot meaning
you don’t have to fully pamper it. Of course you would need
to pay for the expenses of their necessities but they can use
the litter box on their own, eat on their own and explore a
bunch of different places.











Cons

They tend to scratch a lot

Cats have very sharp claws so if you were to
play with them too much or get them angry you might
end up with a huge scar. Not only can they scratch
you but they can also claw at your furniture or work
and end up getting those ruined too. So that might be
something to consider when deciding on owning a cat or
not.




Can be pretty expensive to buy and take care of

Most domestic animals/pets are expensive to buy and
take care of in Canada and cats are definitely not an
exception. From the cost of just being able to own them
to the money being spent on trying to provide for them,
it’s like having a whole new family member except as a pet!








They shed A LOT

By shedding a lot cats can leave a mess in many
people’s houses and it can be ANYWHERE. Also
something to consider is that some may experience an
allergic reaction to cat fur and it may become severe
especially because a lot of cats are friendly with humans.










Your cat may get lost

Like mentioned before, cats are very independent animals
and like to roam around by themselves. With this comes the
risks of losing them as they can be anywhere without you knowing,
maybe something happened to them. However, you’ll never truly know
and it can get concerning at times to know whether or no they’d
come back.








They can keep you up at night by meowing a lot

Cats can be like babies and wake you up at night.
They meow very loudly and a lot of the time they do
it constantly so you’re bound to wake up and check
out what’s going on. This can get very irritating
because you might not know how to help it yet it’d
still be continuously making noise.
